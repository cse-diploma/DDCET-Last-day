UNIT 01

📏 Units & Measurement

⚡ Quick Tips & Tricks
  • SI → CGS: Length ×100 (m→cm), Mass ×1000 (kg→g), Force ×10⁵ (N→dyne), Pressure ×10 (Pa→dyne/cm²)
  • Least Count (LC) of Vernier = 1 MSD − 1 VSD = S/n (S = main scale division, n = Vernier divisions)
  • Micrometer screw gauge LC = Pitch / No. of divisions on circular scale (usually 0.01 mm)
  • % Error = (Δx/x) × 100; for product/quotient: add relative errors
  • For power: multiply relative error by the power (e.g., A³ → 3×Δa/a)

Screw Gauge

Micrometer Screw Gauge

Pitch = Distance / No. of rotations
LC = Pitch / Circular scale divisions
Reading = MSR + (CSR × LC) ± Zero error

Standard: Pitch = 0.5mm, 50 div → LC = 0.01mm = 0.001cm

📋 MCQ High-Yield Points
  • Vernier caliper LC = 0.1 mm (standard), Screw gauge LC = 0.01 mm
  • Systematic error → always same direction; Random error → varies in direction
  • For z = a²b/√c → Δz/z = 2Δa/a + Δb/b + ½Δc/c
  • Fundamental units: m, kg, s, A, K, mol, cd (7 SI base units)
  • 1 N = 10⁵ dyne is the most-asked conversion
  • Zero error: Positive zero error → subtract from reading; Negative → add
  • Backlash error occurs in screw gauge when rotation direction reverses
UNIT 02

⚙️ Classical Mechanics

⚡ Quick Tips & Tricks
  • Remember SUVAT: s=ut+½at², v=u+at, v²=u²+2as, s=(u+v)/2·t
  • At max height of projectile: vertical velocity = 0; horizontal velocity unchanged
  • Centripetal force always points INWARD (toward center); Centrifugal is pseudo (outward)
  • KE = p²/2m (great shortcut when momentum is given)
  • Power = Force × velocity (P = Fv) when force and velocity are parallel
  • Work-energy theorem: Net Work = ΔKE
  • Impulse = Change in momentum = FΔt = Δp

🚀 Fast Calculation Tricks

When momentum p is given and you need KE:

KE = p²/2m

When angular data is given, relate to linear:

v = ωr, a = αr, s = θr

Energy conservation shortcut (no friction): mgh = ½mv² → v = √(2gh)

v = √(2gh) ≈ √(20h) for g=10
📋 MCQ High-Yield Points
  • Newton's 1st law = Law of Inertia; 3rd law = Action-Reaction (equal & opposite)
  • Centripetal force is NOT a new force — it is provided by tension, gravity, normal force etc.
  • Centrifugal force is a pseudo/fictitious force felt in rotating (non-inertial) frame
  • Conservation of momentum: total p is constant when no external force acts
  • For elastic collision: both KE and momentum conserved
  • Unit of power = Watt = J/s = kg·m²/s³
  • 1 HP (horsepower) = 746 W
  • 1 kWh = 3.6 × 10⁶ J (unit of energy on electricity bill)
  • Work done by centripetal force = 0 (force ⊥ displacement)
UNIT 03

⚡ Electric Current

⚡ Quick Tips & Tricks
  • Ohm's Law: V = IR → V/I=R; I=V/R; R=V/I. Always consistent unit: V, A, Ω
  • Series: R_total = R1+R2+... (same current); Parallel: 1/R = 1/R1+1/R2+... (same voltage)
  • For 2 parallel resistors: R_eq = R1×R2/(R1+R2) — "product over sum"
  • Capacitors: OPPOSITE rules to resistors! Series: 1/C = 1/C1+1/C2; Parallel: C = C1+C2
  • Electric field E = F/q = V/d (between parallel plates)
  • Coulomb constant k = 9×10⁹ N·m²/C²; ε₀ = 8.85×10⁻¹² F/m

🔌 Resistor/Capacitor Memory Trick

R & C are OPPOSITE:

Series: R adds up | C splits (1/C) Parallel: R splits (1/R) | C adds up

Think of it: capacitors prefer to be in parallel, resistors prefer series for combination.

📋 MCQ High-Yield Points
  • Coulomb's constant k = 9×10⁹ N·m²/C² = 1/4πε₀
  • ε₀ = 8.85×10⁻¹² F/m (permittivity of free space)
  • Like charges repel; unlike charges attract (Coulomb's law)
  • Electric potential is scalar; Electric field is vector
  • Inside a conductor: E = 0, potential is constant throughout
  • Unit of charge: Coulomb (C); smallest charge = 1.6×10⁻¹⁹ C (electron)
  • Current flows from high to low potential (conventional); electrons move opposite
  • Ohmic conductors: V vs I is a straight line through origin
UNIT 04

🌡️ Heat & Thermometry

⚡ Quick Tips & Tricks — Temperature Conversions
  • K = °C + 273 (or 273.15) — Most common conversion
  • °F = (9/5)°C + 32 → TRICK: multiply °C by 1.8 then add 32
  • °C = (°F − 32) × 5/9 → TRICK: subtract 32 then multiply by 5/9
  • At −40°: Celsius = Fahrenheit (they are equal at −40)
  • At 0 K = −273.15°C = Absolute Zero (lowest possible temperature)
  • Human body: 37°C = 98.6°F = 310 K

🌡️ Temperature Conversion Memory Trick

Scale comparison formula (universal):

(C − 0)/100 = (F − 32)/180 = (K − 273)/100 = (R − 0)/80

Remember: α, β, γ relationship:

α : β : γ = 1 : 2 : 3

Thermal conductivity unit trick: K is in W/mK — "W for Watt, m for meter, K for Kelvin"

📋 MCQ High-Yield Points
  • Specific heat of water = 4200 J/(kg·K) = 1 cal/(g·°C) — highest among common substances
  • Modes of heat transfer: Conduction (solid), Convection (fluid), Radiation (no medium needed)
  • Radiation is the fastest mode; it travels at speed of light
  • Good conductor of heat = bad conductor of electricity is FALSE (metals are good for both)
  • 1 cal = 4.2 J (Joule's mechanical equivalent of heat)
  • Absolute zero = 0 K = −273.15°C; matter has minimum energy at 0 K
  • Latent heat of fusion (ice→water) = 80 cal/g = 336 kJ/kg
  • Latent heat of vaporization (water→steam) = 540 cal/g = 2260 kJ/kg
UNIT 05

🌊 Waves, Optics & Acoustics

⚡ Quick Tips & Tricks
  • Wave speed: v = fλ = λ/T (frequency × wavelength)
  • Snell's law: n₁sin θ₁ = n₂sin θ₂ → for TIR: angle > critical angle C, where sin C = n₂/n₁ (n₂ < n₁)
  • Optical fiber works on Total Internal Reflection (TIR); light must hit at angle > critical angle
  • Reverberation time: Sabine's formula RT₆₀ = 0.161·V/A
  • Sound speed in air ≈ 340 m/s at 20°C; light speed = 3×10⁸ m/s
  • LASER = Light Amplification by Stimulated Emission of Radiation

Wave Types

Types of Waves — Key Distinctions

Transverse: vibration ⊥ propagation (light, water)
Longitudinal: vibration ∥ propagation (sound, P-waves)
Mechanical: needs medium (sound)
Non-mechanical: no medium (light, EM waves)
Progressive: energy transfer; Stationary: no net energy
EM Spectrum

EM Waves (Increasing Wavelength)

γ-rays → X-rays → UV → Visible → IR → Microwave → Radio

All EM waves travel at c = 3×10⁸ m/s in vacuum

Ultrasonic: f > 20,000 Hz; Audible: 20–20,000 Hz; Infrasonic: f < 20 Hz
Optical Fibre

Optical Fibre — TIR Principle

Construction: Core (high n) + Cladding (low n) + Buffer coating

TIR condition: angle of incidence > Critical angle C
sin C = n_cladding / n_core

Applications: Internet cables, medical endoscopes, sensors

Acoustics

Reverberation & Sabine's Formula

RT₆₀ = 0.161 × V / ΣaS

V = volume (m³), a = absorption coeff, S = surface area (m²)

Good speech: RT ≈ 0.5–1.0 s; Good music: RT ≈ 1.5–2.5 s

Echo: sound reflects with ≥ 0.1 s delay → min 17m distance
📋 MCQ High-Yield Points
  • Sound is a LONGITUDINAL wave; Light is a TRANSVERSE wave
  • Sound CANNOT travel in vacuum; Light CAN (EM wave, no medium needed)
  • TIR only occurs when: (1) light goes from denser to rarer medium AND (2) angle > critical angle
  • Refractive index of glass ≈ 1.5; water ≈ 1.33; diamond ≈ 2.42 (highest → smallest critical angle)
  • LASER properties: Monochromatic, Coherent, Highly directional, High intensity
  • Ultrasonic waves: used in SONAR, medical imaging, cleaning, welding
  • Reverberation time too long → sound becomes muddy; too short → sound is too dead
  • Open window has absorption coefficient = 1 (perfect absorber) — defines the Sabin unit
  • In optical fibre: information travels as pulses of light (digital signal)
