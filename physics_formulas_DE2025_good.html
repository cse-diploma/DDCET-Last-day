Units & Measurement

Physical quantities, unit systems, errors and measuring instruments

💡 PYQ Alert: Questions on significant figures, least count of vernier/screw gauge, and percentage error propagation appear every year. Master these formulas well.
Fundamental & Derived Quantities
Density Mechanics
ρ = m / V

Density equals mass divided by volume. CGS: g/cm³, SI: kg/m³. Water density = 1 g/cm³ = 1000 kg/m³.

SI: kg/m³
Volume of a Sphere Geometry
V = (4/3) π r³

Used when finding density of spherical objects. Relates to error propagation in r.

SI: m³

Error Analysis
Mean Absolute Error Error
Δa_mean = (Σ|aᵢ - a_mean|) / n

Average of absolute deviations from the mean value.

Unit: Same as quantity
Relative Error Error
Relative Error = Δa / a_mean

Ratio of mean absolute error to the mean value. Dimensionless.

Unit: Dimensionless
Percentage Error Error
% Error = (Δa / a_mean) × 100%

Relative error expressed as a percentage.

Unit: %
Error in Sum / Difference Propagation
If Z = A ± B
ΔZ = ΔA + ΔB

Absolute errors always ADD, regardless of + or −.

Rule: Add absolute errors
Error in Product / Quotient Propagation
If Z = AⁿBᵐ
ΔZ/Z = n(ΔA/A) + m(ΔB/B)

Relative errors are multiplied by their respective powers and added. Key for density of sphere: Δρ/ρ = Δm/m + 3(Δr/r).

Rule: Add relative errors × powers
% Error in Density of Sphere ★ PYQ
%Δρ = %Δm + 3 × %Δr

Since ρ = m/V = m/(4/3 πr³), volume has power 3 for radius. E.g., if Δm=0.3%, Δr=0.5% → Δρ = 0.3 + 3×0.5 = 1.8%

Unit: %
Vernier Caliper
Least Count — Vernier Caliper Instrument
L.C. = 1 MSD − 1 VSD
L.C. = S / N

S = smallest main scale division, N = number of vernier scale divisions. Typical: 1 MSD = 1mm, N = 10 → L.C. = 0.1mm.

Typical L.C.: 0.1 mm
Vernier Reading Instrument
Reading = MSR + (VSR × L.C.)

MSR = Main Scale Reading, VSR = Vernier Scale Reading (coinciding division number).

Unit: mm or cm
Micrometer Screw Gauge
Least Count — Screw Gauge ★ PYQ
L.C. = Pitch / N

Pitch = distance moved per revolution, N = divisions on circular scale. PYQ: Pitch = 1mm, N = 100 → L.C. = 0.01mm = 0.001cm.

Typical L.C.: 0.01 mm
Screw Gauge Reading Instrument
Reading = LSR + (CSR × L.C.)

LSR = Linear Scale Reading, CSR = Circular Scale Reading. Zero error must be subtracted if present.

Unit: mm
Significant Figures Rules

Key Rules

Rule 1 All non-zero digits are significant
Rule 2 Zeros between non-zeros are significant
Rule 3 Leading zeros are NOT significant
Rule 4 Trailing zeros after decimal ARE significant

Classical Mechanics

Linear motion, Newton's laws, circular motion, work-energy-power

💡 PYQ Alert: Force = ma, centripetal acceleration, angular velocity formula and momentum are frequently tested. See Q31, Q32 from 2025 paper.
Linear Motion — Kinematics
Average Velocity Kinematics
v = s / t

Displacement per unit time.

SI: m/s
Acceleration Kinematics
a = (v − u) / t

Rate of change of velocity. u = initial velocity, v = final velocity.

SI: m/s²
Three Equations of Motion ★ Key
v = u + at
s = ut + ½at²
v² = u² + 2as

u = initial vel, v = final vel, a = acceleration, s = displacement, t = time. Valid for uniform acceleration.

SI: m, m/s, m/s²
Newton's Laws & Force
Newton's 2nd Law — Force ★ PYQ
F = m × a

Force = mass × acceleration. PYQ: m = 10 kg, a = 9.8 m/s² → F = 98 N.

SI: Newton (N) = kg·m/s²
Linear Momentum Newton
p = m × v

Product of mass and velocity. A vector quantity. Newton's 2nd law: F = dp/dt.

SI: kg·m/s
Impulse Newton
J = F × t = Δp = m(v − u)

Impulse equals change in momentum. Equal to area under F-t graph.

SI: N·s = kg·m/s
Weight Newton
W = m × g

g = 9.8 m/s² (acceleration due to gravity). Weight is a force, measured in Newtons.

SI: Newton (N)
Circular Motion
Angular Velocity ★ PYQ
ω = v / r

Also: ω = 2π/T = 2πf. Relation between linear speed v, radius r and angular velocity ω.

SI: rad/s
Centripetal Acceleration ★ PYQ
a꜀ = v² / r = ω²r

Directed toward center. PYQ: v = 60 m/s, r = 2 m → a꜀ = 3600/2 = 1800 m/s².

SI: m/s²
Centripetal Force Circular
F꜀ = mv² / r = mω²r

Force required to keep a body in circular motion. Always acts toward center. Centrifugal force = reaction, acts away from center.

SI: Newton (N)
Angular Acceleration Circular
α = (ω₂ − ω₁) / t

Rate of change of angular velocity. Related to linear: a = α·r.

SI: rad/s²
Period & Frequency (Circular) Circular
T = 2π / ω = 1 / f

T = time for one full revolution, f = number of revolutions per second.

T: s  |  f: Hz
Work, Energy & Power
Work Done Energy
W = F × d × cos θ

θ = angle between force and displacement. W = Fd when force is along displacement (θ=0).

SI: Joule (J)
Kinetic Energy Energy
KE = ½ mv²

Energy due to motion. Also: KE = p²/(2m). Work-energy theorem: W = ΔKE.

SI: Joule (J)
Gravitational Potential Energy Energy
PE = m × g × h

Energy due to position. h = height above reference level. Conservation: KE + PE = constant.

SI: Joule (J)
Power Energy
P = W / t = F × v

Rate of doing work. Also P = F·v for constant force. 1 HP = 746 W.

SI: Watt (W) = J/s

Electric Current

Charge, Coulomb's law, Ohm's law, capacitance, resistors

💡 PYQ Alert: Coulomb's law (inverse square), series resistance, capacitance with dielectric, and number of electrons per coulomb appear frequently. See Q35–Q38.
Charge & Coulomb's Law
Coulomb's Law ★ PYQ
F = k·q₁·q₂ / r²
k = 9×10⁹ N·m²/C²

Force is inversely proportional to square of distance. If r is doubled → F becomes 1/4th.

SI: Newton (N)
Charge of Electron & Coulomb ★ PYQ
e = 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ C
n = Q / e
1 C = 6.25 × 10¹⁸ electrons

PYQ: Q35 directly asked how many electrons in 1 coulomb → 6.25 × 10¹⁸.

SI: Coulomb (C)
Electric Field, Potential & Flux
Electric Field Field
E = F / q = kQ / r²

Force per unit charge. Due to point charge Q at distance r. Field lines are imaginary (PYQ Q34).

SI: N/C or V/m
Electric Potential Field
V = W / q = kQ / r

Work done per unit charge to bring a positive test charge from infinity.

SI: Volt (V) = J/C
Electric Flux ★ PYQ
Φ = E × A × cos θ
Gauss: Φ = Q_enc / ε₀

PYQ Q33: SI unit of electric flux = N·m²/C (or V·m).

SI: N·m²/C
Ohm's Law & Resistance
Ohm's Law ★ Key
V = I × R

Voltage = Current × Resistance. Valid for ohmic conductors at constant temperature.

V: V  |  I: A  |  R: Ω
Resistance & Resistivity Resistance
R = ρ × L / A

ρ = resistivity, L = length, A = cross-sectional area. Conductance G = 1/R.

R: Ω  |  ρ: Ω·m
Resistors in Series ★ PYQ
R_eq = R₁ + R₂ + R₃ + ...

Total resistance increases. Same current through all. PYQ: 5+10+15 = 30Ω.

SI: Ohm (Ω)
Resistors in Parallel Resistance
1/R_eq = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂ + 1/R₃

Equivalent resistance is less than smallest individual resistance. Same voltage across all.

SI: Ohm (Ω)
Capacitance
Capacitance Capacitor
C = Q / V

Ability to store charge per unit voltage.

SI: Farad (F) = C/V
Parallel Plate Capacitor ★ PYQ
C = ε₀·εᵣ·A / d

PYQ Q36: Inserting dielectric (εᵣ > 1) INCREASES capacitance. A = area, d = separation, εᵣ = relative permittivity.

SI: Farad (F)
Capacitors in Series Capacitor
1/C_eq = 1/C₁ + 1/C₂ + 1/C₃

Equivalent capacitance is less than smallest. Same charge on each.

SI: Farad (F)
Capacitors in Parallel Capacitor
C_eq = C₁ + C₂ + C₃ + ...

Equivalent capacitance increases. Same voltage across each.

SI: Farad (F)
Energy Stored in Capacitor Capacitor
U = ½CV² = ½QV = Q²/2C

Energy stored in electric field between the plates.

SI: Joule (J)
Current & Power
Electric Current Current
I = Q / t

Rate of flow of charge. Conventional current flows from + to −.

SI: Ampere (A) = C/s
Electrical Power Power
P = VI = I²R = V²/R

Rate of electrical energy consumption. Three equivalent forms using Ohm's law.

SI: Watt (W)

Heat & Thermometry

Temperature scales, heat capacity, thermal conductivity, expansion

💡 PYQ Alert: Temperature conversion (37°C = 98.6°F), 1 KCal = 4186 J, SI unit of thermal conductivity, and specific heat definition are directly tested. See Q40–Q44.
Temperature Scale Conversions
°C ↔ °F Conversion ★ PYQ
°F = (9/5 × °C) + 32
°C = (°F − 32) × 5/9

PYQ: 37°C → °F = (9/5 × 37) + 32 = 66.6 + 32 = 98.6°F.

Note: Body temp = 37°C = 98.6°F
°C ↔ Kelvin Conversion Conversion
K = °C + 273.15
°C = K − 273.15

Absolute zero = 0 K = −273.15°C. Kelvin is the SI unit of thermodynamic temperature.

SI: Kelvin (K)
Kelvin ↔ °F Conversion Conversion
°F = (9/5 × (K − 273)) + 32

Convert K to °C first, then to °F. Or use: K = (5/9)(°F − 32) + 273.

SI: Kelvin (K)
Scale Comparison Formula Conversion
(C − 0)/100 = (F − 32)/180 = (K − 273)/100

General relation between all three scales. C/5 = (F−32)/9 = (K−273)/5.

Key values: 0°C = 32°F = 273K
Heat, Specific Heat & Capacity
Heat Absorbed/Released ★ Key
Q = m × c × ΔT

m = mass, c = specific heat capacity, ΔT = temperature change.

SI: Joule (J)
Specific Heat Capacity ★ PYQ
c = Q / (m × ΔT)

Heat per unit mass per unit temperature rise. Water: c = 4186 J/kg·K = 1 cal/g·°C. PYQ Q39: Also called "specific heat".

SI: J/(kg·K)
Heat Capacity (Thermal Capacity) Heat
H = m × c = Q / ΔT

Heat required per unit temperature rise for the entire body (not per unit mass).

SI: J/K
Energy Unit Conversion ★ PYQ
1 cal = 4.186 J
1 KCal = 4186 J
1 J = 10⁷ erg

PYQ Q23 & Q40 directly. 1 kcal = 1000 cal = 4186 J.

SI: Joule (J)
Latent Heat & Phase Change
Latent Heat ★ PYQ
Q = m × L

Heat absorbed/released during phase change at constant temperature. PYQ Q42: Boiling water at 100°C → temperature remains constant while heat is supplied (phase change).

SI: J/kg
Thermal Conductivity & Expansion
Fourier's Law — Heat Conduction ★ PYQ
Q/t = −k × A × (ΔT/Δx)

k = coefficient of thermal conductivity. PYQ Q44: SI unit of k = W/(m·K).

SI of k: W/(m·K)
Linear Thermal Expansion Expansion
ΔL = α × L₀ × ΔT
L = L₀(1 + αΔT)

α = coefficient of linear expansion. Different for different materials. PYQ Q41: Glass cracks on cooling due to low thermal conductivity (uneven cooling).

α: K⁻¹ or °C⁻¹
Volume (Cubical) Expansion Expansion
ΔV = γ × V₀ × ΔT
γ = 3α

γ = coefficient of volume expansion = 3 times the linear expansion coefficient.

γ: K⁻¹
Modes of Heat Transfer Transfer
Stefan's Law: P = σAT⁴
σ = 5.67 × 10⁻⁸ W/m²K⁴

Conduction: through solids (Q/t = kAΔT/L). Convection: through fluids. Radiation: no medium, Stefan's law. Newton's cooling: dT/dt ∝ (T − T₀).

SI: W/m²

Waves, Optics & Acoustics

Wave motion, LASER, optical fiber, reverberation, refraction

💡 PYQ Alert: Wave formula (v = fλ), period of 20 Hz, speed of light, reverberation (Sabine's formula), audible frequency range, and numerical aperture of optical fiber — all appeared directly.
Wave Fundamentals
Wave Speed ★ PYQ
v = f × λ = λ / T

v = speed, f = frequency, λ = wavelength, T = time period. Fundamental wave equation.

v: m/s  |  f: Hz  |  λ: m
Time Period & Frequency ★ PYQ
T = 1 / f

PYQ Q47: f = 20 Hz → T = 1/20 = 0.05 sec.

T: second (s)  |  f: Hz
Wave Equation Waves
y = A sin(ωt − kx)
ω = 2πf = 2π/T
k = 2π/λ

A = amplitude, ω = angular frequency, k = wave number, x = position, t = time.

ω: rad/s  |  k: rad/m
Intensity of Wave Waves
I ∝ A² ∝ f²A²

Intensity proportional to square of amplitude. I = P/(4πr²) for point source.

SI: W/m²

Optics — Refraction & Snell's Law
Snell's Law ★ Key
n₁ sin θ₁ = n₂ sin θ₂
n = c / v

n₁, n₂ = refractive indices. θ₁ = angle of incidence. Light bends toward normal when entering denser medium.

n: Dimensionless
Refractive Index Optics
n = c / v = sin i / sin r

Absolute: ratio of speed of light in vacuum to speed in medium. Relative: n₂₁ = n₂/n₁.

Unit: Dimensionless
Critical Angle & TIR Optics
sin θ꜀ = n₂ / n₁ = 1/n
TIR: θ > θ꜀ (from denser to rarer)

When light hits denser→rarer boundary at angle > critical angle, it is totally reflected. Basis of optical fiber.

θ꜀: degrees or radians
Optical Fiber
Numerical Aperture (NA) ★ PYQ
NA = √(n₁² − n₂²)

PYQ Q50: n₁ = refractive index of core, n₂ = refractive index of cladding. n₁ > n₂ for TIR to occur.

Unit: Dimensionless
Acceptance Angle Fiber
sin θₐ = NA = √(n₁² − n₂²)

Maximum angle at which light enters the fiber and undergoes TIR. θₐ = acceptance half-angle.

θₐ: degrees
Acoustics — Reverberation & Echo
Sabine's Reverberation Formula ★ PYQ
T = 0.161 × V / A

T = reverberation time (s), V = volume of hall (m³), A = total absorption (OWU = m²). PYQ Q49: V=5000, A=900 → T = 0.161×5000/900 ≈ 0.894 s.

T: second (s)
Echo — Minimum Distance Acoustics
d = v × t / 2
d_min = 17 m (at 25°C)

Sound must travel to reflector and back. Minimum 17 m for echo to be perceived (persistence of hearing ≈ 0.1 s).

SI: metre (m)
Absorption Coefficient Acoustics
α = Sound absorbed / Sound incident
A = Σ aᵢ Sᵢ (OWU)

Ranges 0 (perfect reflector) to 1 (perfect absorber). Total absorption A in Open Window Units (m²).

Unit: OWU (m²)

LASER = Light Amplification by
Stimulated Emission of Radiation

PYQ Q48: LASER is (D) All of above — unidirectional, focused, coherent and stimulated. Key: monochromatic, highly collimated, coherent.

Type: Electromagnetic wave
