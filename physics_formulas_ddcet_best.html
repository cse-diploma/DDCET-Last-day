Error Analysis Formulas
Absolute Error (each reading)
Δaᵢ = |aₘₑₐₙ - aᵢ|
aₘₑₐₙ = (a₁+a₂+...+aₙ)/n
Mean Absolute Error
Δā = (Δa₁+Δa₂+...+Δaₙ)/n
Relative Error (Fractional Error)
Relative Error = Δā / aₘₑₐₙ
Percentage Error
% Error = (Δā / aₘₑₐₙ) × 100%
Error in Sum / Difference
Z = A ± B → ΔZ = ΔA + ΔB
Absolute errors always ADD
Error in Product / Quotient
Z = A·B or A/B
ΔZ/Z = ΔA/A + ΔB/B
Relative errors add
Error in Power (Z = Aᵐ·Bⁿ / Cᵖ)
ΔZ/Z = m(ΔA/A) + n(ΔB/B) + p(ΔC/C)
Sphere Density % Error
ρ = m/V = 3m/(4πr³)
Δρ/ρ = Δm/m + 3(Δr/r)
Measuring Instruments
Vernier Caliper — Least Count
LC = 1 MSD − 1 VSD
LC = S / N
S = value of 1 MSD; N = number of VSD divisions. Typically 0.1 mm or 0.02 mm.
Vernier Caliper — Reading
Reading = MSR + (VSR × LC)
MSR = Main Scale Reading; VSR = Vernier Scale Reading (coinciding division)
Micrometer Screw Gauge — Least Count
LC = Pitch / No. of divisions
LC = p / n
Typical: pitch=0.5mm, n=50 → LC=0.01mm
Micrometer — Reading
Reading = PSR + (HSR × LC) ± ZE
PSR = Pitch Scale; HSR = Head Scale; ZE = Zero Error
Significant Figures — Rules
Non-zero digits: always significant
Zeros between non-zero: significant
Leading zeros: NOT significant
Trailing zeros (decimal): significant

Classical Mechanics
Linear Motion — Equations of Motion
1st Equation (velocity-time)
v = u + at
v,u → m/s  |  a → m/s²  |  t → s
2nd Equation (position-time)
s = ut + ½at²
s → m
3rd Equation (velocity-position)
v² = u² + 2as
Average Velocity
v_avg = (u + v) / 2
v_avg = total displacement / time
Distance in nth second
sₙ = u + a(2n − 1)/2
Newton's Laws of Motion
Newton's 1st Law (Inertia)
F_net = 0 → a = 0 (body at rest or uniform motion)
Newton's 2nd Law
F = ma
F = dp/dt
F → N (newton)  |  m → kg  |  a → m/s²
Newton's 3rd Law
F_AB = −F_BA
Action and reaction are equal & opposite
Weight
W = mg
W → N  |  g = 9.8 m/s²
Friction Force
f = μN
μ = coefficient of friction, N = normal force
Linear Momentum & Impulse
Linear Momentum
p = mv
SI: kg·m/s  |  CGS: g·cm/s
Law of Conservation of Momentum
p₁ + p₂ = p₁' + p₂' (constant when F_ext=0)
Impulse of Force
J = F × t
J = Δp = m(v − u)
SI: N·s or kg·m/s
Impulse-Momentum Theorem
F·Δt = Δ(mv)
Circular Motion
Angular Velocity
ω = v / r
ω = 2πn = 2π/T
SI: rad/s
Angular Acceleration
α = Δω / Δt
SI: rad/s²
Centripetal Acceleration
a_c = v² / r = ω²r
SI: m/s²
Centripetal Force
F_c = mv² / r = mω²r
SI: N  |  directed TOWARDS centre
Centrifugal Force (pseudo)
F_cf = mv² / r
Equal in magnitude to centripetal, directed AWAY from centre
Time Period & Frequency
T = 2πr / v = 2π / ω
n = 1/T
Relation: v, ω, r
v = ωr
Work, Energy & Power
Work Done
W = F·d·cosθ
SI: joule (J)  |  CGS: erg
Kinetic Energy
KE = ½mv²
KE = p² / (2m)
SI: J
Potential Energy (Gravitational)
PE = mgh
SI: J
Work-Energy Theorem
W_net = ΔKE = KE_f − KE_i
Conservation of Mechanical Energy
KE + PE = constant
½mv² + mgh = constant
Power
P = W / t = F·v·cosθ
SI: watt (W)  |  1 hp = 746 W
Work by external forces
W_ext = ΔE_total = ΔKE + ΔPE

Electric Current
Electric Charge & Coulomb's Law
Coulomb's Force
F = kq₁q₂ / r²
k = 1/(4πε₀) = 9×10⁹ N·m²/C²
SI: N  |  q → C  |  r → m
Permittivity of free space
ε₀ = 8.85×10⁻¹² C²/N·m²
Unit: C²/(N·m²)
Charge Quantisation
q = ne
e = 1.6×10⁻¹⁹ C; n = number of electrons
Electrons in 1 Coulomb
n = 1/e = 6.25×10¹⁸
Electric Field & Potential
Electric Field Intensity
E = F / q
E = kQ / r²
SI: N/C or V/m
Electric Potential
V = W / q
V = kQ / r
SI: volt (V) = J/C
Relation E and V
E = −dV/dr
Field points in direction of decreasing potential
Electric Flux
Φ = E·A·cosθ
SI: N·m²/C or V·m
Gauss's Law
Φ = Q_enc / ε₀
Ohm's Law & Resistance
Ohm's Law
V = IR
I = V/R    R = V/I
V → volt  |  I → ampere  |  R → ohm (Ω)
Resistance
R = ρL / A
ρ = resistivity (Ω·m)  |  L = length (m)  |  A = area (m²)
Conductance
G = 1/R
SI: siemens (S) or mho (℧)
Conductivity
σ = 1/ρ
SI: S/m or (Ω·m)⁻¹
Resistors in Series
R_eq = R₁ + R₂ + R₃ + ...
Same current through each; voltage divides
Resistors in Parallel
1/R_eq = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂ + 1/R₃
For 2: R_eq = R₁R₂/(R₁+R₂)
Same voltage; current divides
Electric Power
P = VI = I²R = V²/R
SI: W (watt)
Electrical Energy
E = P·t = VIt = I²Rt
SI: J (joule)  |  Commercial: kWh
Capacitance & Capacitors
Capacitance
C = Q / V
SI: farad (F) = C/V
Parallel Plate Capacitor
C = ε₀·A / d
C = ε₀εᵣ·A / d (with dielectric)
Dielectric increases capacitance by factor εᵣ
Capacitors in Series
1/C_eq = 1/C₁ + 1/C₂ + ...
Capacitors in Parallel
C_eq = C₁ + C₂ + C₃ + ...
Energy stored in Capacitor
U = ½CV² = Q²/(2C) = QV/2
SI: J

Heat & Thermometry
Temperature Scale Conversions
Celsius ↔ Fahrenheit
°F = (9/5)°C + 32
°C = (5/9)(°F − 32)
Equal at: −40°C = −40°F
Celsius ↔ Kelvin
K = °C + 273.15
°C = K − 273.15
Fahrenheit ↔ Kelvin
K = (5/9)(°F − 32) + 273.15
General Scale Relation
(C−0)/100 = (F−32)/180 = (K−273)/100
Heat Capacity & Specific Heat
Heat Energy
Q = mcΔT
Q → J  |  m → kg  |  c → J/(kg·K)  |  ΔT → K
Specific Heat Capacity
c = Q / (mΔT)
SI: J/(kg·K)  |  CGS: cal/(g·°C)
Heat Capacity (of body)
C = mc = Q/ΔT
SI: J/K  |  Also: cal/°C or Cal/K
Latent Heat
Q = mL
L → J/kg  |  L_fusion(water)=3.36×10⁵ J/kg
Unit Conversion
1 cal = 4.186 J
1 kcal = 4186 J = 4.186 kJ
Principle of Calorimetry
Heat lost = Heat gained
m₁c₁ΔT₁ = m₂c₂ΔT₂
Thermal Expansion
Linear Expansion
ΔL = αLΔT
L_f = L₀(1 + αΔT)
α = linear expansion coeff. (K⁻¹ or °C⁻¹)
Area Expansion
ΔA = βAΔT
β = 2α
Volume Expansion
ΔV = γVΔT
γ = 3α
γ = volume expansion coeff.
Relation between coefficients
β = 2α    γ = 3α
Thermal Conductivity
Fourier's Law (Heat Conduction)
Q/t = kA(T₁−T₂)/d
k = thermal conductivity coeff.
k → W/(m·K)
Coefficient of Thermal Conductivity
k = Qd / [A·t·(T₁−T₂)]
SI: W/(m·K) or J/(m·s·K)
Newton's Law of Cooling
dT/dt = −k(T − T₀)
T = body temp; T₀ = surrounding temp
Stefan-Boltzmann Law (Radiation)
P = εσAT⁴
σ = 5.67×10⁻⁸ W/(m²·K⁴); ε = emissivity
Thermal Conductivity Order
Ag > Cu > Al > Fe (metals)
Silver has highest; Iron has lowest among common metals

Wave Motion, Optics & Acoustics
Wave Fundamentals
Wave Speed Relation
v = fλ = λ/T
v → m/s  |  f → Hz  |  λ → m  |  T → s
Frequency & Time Period
f = 1/T
ω = 2πf = 2π/T
f → Hz  |  T → s  |  ω → rad/s
Angular Wave Number
k = 2π/λ
k → rad/m
Wave Equation
y = A sin(ωt − kx)
y = A sin(2πft − 2πx/λ)
A = amplitude (m)
Intensity of Wave
I ∝ A²f²
I = P / (4πr²) (for point source)
SI: W/m²
Sound Waves & Frequency Ranges
Speed of Sound (in medium)
v = √(B/ρ) (liquids/solids)
v = √(γP/ρ) (gases)
v_air ≈ 340 m/s at 20°C
Speed of Sound vs Temperature
v_T = v₀√(T/T₀)
v_T ≈ 331 + 0.61t m/s (t in °C)
Audible Sound Range
20 Hz ≤ f ≤ 20,000 Hz (20 kHz)
Infrasonic
f < 20 Hz
Ultrasonic
f > 20,000 Hz (20 kHz)
Doppler Effect (source moving)
f' = f₀ × (v ± v_o) / (v ∓ v_s)
+ when approaching; − when receding
Light & Electromagnetic Waves
Speed of Light (vacuum)
c = 3 × 10⁸ m/s
EM Wave — Speed in medium
v = c / n
n = refractive index of medium
Absolute Refractive Index
n = c / v
n = sin i / sin r (Snell's Law)
Snell's Law
n₁ sin θ₁ = n₂ sin θ₂
n₁/n₂ = sin θ₂/sin θ₁ = v₁/v₂ = λ₁/λ₂
Relative Refractive Index
₁n₂ = n₂/n₁ = v₁/v₂
Critical Angle (TIR condition)
sin θ_c = n₂/n₁ (n₁ > n₂)
sin θ_c = 1/n (n₂=1, air)
Total Internal Reflection
θ_i > θ_c AND n₁ > n₂
Basis of optical fibre; light stays inside denser medium
Lens Formula
1/f = 1/v − 1/u
f → m (focal length)  |  Sign convention applies
Magnification (Lens)
m = v/u = h_i/h_o
Mirror Formula
1/f = 1/v + 1/u
f = R/2
Optical Fibre
Numerical Aperture (NA)
NA = √(n₁² − n₂²)
n₁ = core RI; n₂ = cladding RI; n₁ > n₂
Acceptance Angle
sin θ_a = NA = √(n₁² − n₂²)
Principle
TIR: θ > θ_c when n_core > n_cladding
Light propagates by successive total internal reflections
Acoustics — Reverberation & Sabine's Formula
Sabine's Formula (Reverberation Time)
T = 0.161 × V / A
T = reverberation time (s); V = volume (m³); A = total absorption (OWU)
Total Absorption
A = Σ(aᵢ·Sᵢ)
A → OWU (Open Window Unit = m²)
aᵢ = absorption coeff; Sᵢ = surface area (m²)
Absorption Coefficient
a = Sound absorbed / Sound incident
a = 0 (perfect reflector); a = 1 (open window)
Echo Condition
d ≥ 17 m (at 20°C, v=340 m/s)
Min time = 0.1 s for distinct echo
d = v·t/2
Intensity Level (Decibel)
β = 10 log₁₀(I/I₀) dB
I₀ = 10⁻¹² W/m² (threshold of hearing)
LASER Properties

Light Amplification by Stimulated Emission of Radiation
LASER Characteristics
Coherent  |  Monochromatic
Unidirectional  |  High Intensity
NOT divergent — this is the key differentiator (converges)

E = hf = hc/λ
h = 6.63×10⁻³⁴ J·s (Planck's constant)
